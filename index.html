I'm Chance Hoskinson, a PhD student in Physics at the University of Utah working on dark matter, physics beyond the Standard Model, and particle astrophysics.

Explore My Research

Research Interests

My work focuses on dark matter theory and phenomenology, physics beyond the Standard Model, and particle astrophysics and cosmology.

Current Projects

Current projects include gamma-ray background modeling for dark matter physics and likelihood analyses of theoretical and empirical background models using Fermi-LAT data.

Methods & Tools

I build open-source computational tools, perform likelihood-based inference, and develop pipelines that connect relic density and annihilation cross-section calculations to gamma-ray analyses.

Recent Updates

Recent work includes an arXiv preprint (arXiv:2603.05457), a 2025 Phys. Rev. D publication, and invited talks and presentations at workshops and symposia.

About Me

University of Utah, Department of Physics and Astronomy
